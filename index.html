01 — About

Research Agenda

Ieke de Vries is an Assistant Professor at Leiden University, where her work sits at the intersection of criminology, network science, and public health. Her research asks a deceptively simple question: who is exposed to sexual exploitation and human trafficking, and why — and how do the people, places, and institutions around a person shape that risk?

Her agenda spans three connected lines of inquiry: the victimization of minors and adults in trafficking and exploitation contexts; the institutional and policy responses that attempt to identify and address it; and the spatial and social network structures — from city blocks to peer ties — that concentrate or diffuse harm. Across all three, she draws on qualitative, quantitative, and computational methods to work with populations that are, by design, hard to observe.

Before joining Leiden University, she held positions at Northeastern University (Boston) and Florida State University, and was recognized with the 2019 Outstanding Teaching Award from Northeastern's College of Social Sciences and Humanities.
