01 — About

Research Agenda & Affiliations

Ieke de Vries is an Assistant Professor at Leiden University's Institute for Criminal Law & Criminology, where her work sits at the intersection of criminology, network science, and public health. Her research asks who is exposed to sexual exploitation and human trafficking, and why — and how the people, places, and institutions around a person shape that risk.

She coordinates the Faculty of Law's Human Trafficking research cluster, an interdisciplinary group working with national and international partners. She also coordinates Leiden's undergraduate elective course on human trafficking.

Her agenda spans three connected lines of inquiry: the victimization of minors and young adults in cumulative victimization; the institutional and policy responses that attempt to identify and address it; and the spatial and social network structures that concentrate or diffuse harm, studied through both quantitative and qualitative methods.

05 — Ongoing Work

Current projects

Cluster Coordinator

Human Trafficking Research Cluster, Leiden Law School

Ieke de Vries coordinates this interdisciplinary research cluster within the Institute for Criminal Law & Criminology, working with colleagues across Leiden's Faculty of Law and partners including Radboud University, the Violence and Justice Research Lab at Northeastern University, the Trafficking in Persons – Risk to Resilience Lab at the University of South Florida, the University of Leeds, and the Université du Luxembourg. Current projects examine the framing of trafficking in policy and media, the socioecological context of the sexual exploitation of minors, victim compensation, and the life course of identified victims of criminal exploitation.
